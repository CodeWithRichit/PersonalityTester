16 Personalities

The 16 Personality Test, based on the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator, classifies people into sixteen personality types using four preference pairs: Introversion–Extraversion, Sensing–Intuition, Thinking–Feeling, and Judging–Perceiving. It helps individuals understand how they process information, make decisions, interact socially, and approach life situations, improving self-awareness and communication skills.

Big 5 Personalities

The Big Five Personality Model, also known as the Big Five personality traits, measures personality across five dimensions: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, and Neuroticism. Unlike type-based systems, it views personality on a spectrum, making it scientifically reliable for studying behavior, emotional stability, and long-term psychological patterns.
